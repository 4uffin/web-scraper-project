Today’s Stock Market
Michael Reinking, CFASeptember 22, 2025 at 9:00 a.m. ESTFriday was a mixed session capping off a week of gains in US equity markets. It was a big liquidity event with the second largest NYSE closing auction in history measured in both shares and notional value traded at ~3.1B and ~$195B, respectively. With volatility having been sucked out of the market over the last few months traders were waiting to see if the rolling off of positions associated with the expiration would - Make Volatility Great Again (MVGA), kind of like the witching hour of the 1:00 NFL games yesterday. It looked like there might be a changing of the tide ahead of the open after a sharp selloff in the crypto complex overnight which seemed to bleed into some of the recent high flying themes like digital asset treasury companies (DATs), quantum computing and nuclear stocks. US futures were trading modestly lower
but the dip was shallow with buyers quick to show up. Many of the aforementioned momentum stocks were down double digits in percentage terms at the open but outside of the crypto names have recouped most of those losses.
Large caps are outperforming helped by strength in the tech sector.
