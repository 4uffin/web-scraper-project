S&P futures are trading around either side of unchanged, on this, the first official, full day of Fall. The S&P is off its overnight lows while Treasury yields are down slightly across the curve. Global PMIs and more Fed Speak are the main events today.
Equities finished higher yesterday, with small caps outperforming (R2K +0.6%). Yields rose modestly across the curve despite new Fed governor Stephen Miran’s comprehensive analysis and argument for lowering rates. Gold was up 2%, continuing to set record highs, while Bitcoin fell. The M&A news ticker is taking a breather after a very active start to the week: Pfizer for Metsera, Patient Square for Premier, Compass for Anywhere, Thoma Bravo for PROS.
There’s also some earnings today, including AutoZone (before the open) and Micron tonight. President Trump will address the UNGA today around 10am and meet with multiple world leaders, including Zelensky.
Trump is also scheduled to meet with Democratic leaders on Thursday to discuss the looming government shutdown, set to happen in one week. The House passed a CR last week that would extend funding until Nov. 21, then recessed until Oct. 1.
So no more votes from them until after a shutdown would commence. Today’s data is headlined by flash PMIs, with the US data out after the bell.
Chair Powell will speak later today, as will Vice Chair Bowman and Atlanta President Bostic. Bowman’s comments could be of particular interest as she was a dissenting vote at the FOMC meeting before this last one, along with Waller, when they both wanted to cut rates.
Yesterday new Fed Governor Stephen Miran presented his case for more aggressive rate cuts, including an analysis of the neutral interest rate or r*.
He is nothing if not thorough. Brent crude is up 1%. Gold and silver are up similarly, continuing sharp moves higher since Friday.
