What do they have in common? They're both short, with just four letters apiece.When it comes to baby names, it seems that brevity is in. And why wouldn't it be? Short names are often easy ones to deal with, and picturing a lifetime of writing out a name, spelling it for other people, and filling out forms makes a short name seem all the more appealing.For families on the hunt, here are some top short names for baby boys this year. Some are taken from the list of the most popular names of the year put out by the Social Security Administration (SSA). Others still haven't hit that saturation level yet, and are still uncommon enough to feel unique. All of them are short and to the point.Two-Letter Boy NamesInterestingly enough, there are only two boys' names on the SSA list that are only two letters long: Bo and Ty.
