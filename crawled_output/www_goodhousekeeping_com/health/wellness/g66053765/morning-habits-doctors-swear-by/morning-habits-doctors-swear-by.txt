And how you start your morning can set the tone for the entire day — from improving focus and reducing stress to supporting your spine and digestion. Doctors across specialties say they rely on simple yet powerful habits to keep their energy steady, their minds sharp and their bodies strong — and many of these practices take only a few minutes.Whether it’s hydrating before coffee, stepping outside for a dose of daylight or doing a quick stretch before leaving bed, these science-backed routines help prevent health issues down the line while boosting wellbeing right now. “These small things create a bedrock for consistency in wellbeing,” says Andreas Bernhardt M.D., a primary care physician based in Switzerland who specializes in longevity medicine. Here, medical professionals share the morning habits doctors swear by — and how you can easily make them part of your own routine.1Hydrate before anything elseIgor Suka//Getty ImagesDr. Bernhardt starts every day with water to stimulate metabolism and circulation. “It’s the first thing I do before anything else,” he says. Hydrating helps wake up your body after hours without fluids and supports everything from digestion to energy.2Stretch before getting out of bedskynesher//Getty ImagesSpine specialist Taher Saifullah M.D., founder of Spine & Pain Institute Los Angeles recommends gentle stretches like knee-to-chest movements while still lying down. “It warms up the spine and reduces risk of injury,” he says. Try these 10 morning stretches for beginners.Advertisement - Continue Reading Below3Practice gratitudeFlashpop//Getty ImagesKick off your morning with a thankful heart. "Start your day with intention and gratitude," says Phyllis Nsiah-Kumi, M.D., concierge physician at Bespoke Concierge MD located in New York City. "That could be as simple as taking a moment to list three things you’re thankful for before you get out of bed, or writing a single sentence in a journal." This small but powerful practice is important because it "helps you focus on what's good in your life, rather than what you feel you lack or what's stressing you out," says Dr. Nsiah-Kumi.
