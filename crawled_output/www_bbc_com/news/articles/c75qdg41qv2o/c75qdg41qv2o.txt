Cooper, 15, was named best supporting actor in a limited series for his portrayal of a schoolboy accused of murdering a classmate.The four-part drama also won best limited series, as well as prizes for its directing and writing, and acting prizes for Stephen Graham and Erin Doherty.In his acceptance speech, Cooper said: "Standing up here is just, wow, it's just so surreal."When I started these drama classes a couple of years back, I didn't expect to even be in the United States, never mind here."The winners and nominations in fullCash for speeches and big wins for The Pitt and The Studio - 7 Emmys highlightsHow Adolescence pulled off an Emmys sweepIn pictures: Sydney Sweeney, Jenna Ortega and Selena Gomez on red carpetCooper, who played teenager Jamie Miller, said: "I think tonight proves if you listen, and you focus and you step out your comfort zone, you can achieve anything in life. "Who cares if you get embarrassed? Anything can be possible. I was nothing about three years ago, I'm here now."He also thanked his family, co-stars, and the show's production team, adding: "It may have my name on this award, but it really belongs to the people behind the camera."Getty ImagesCooper, 15, described his record-breaking win as "surreal"Cooper broke the record previously held by Scott Jacoby, who was 16 years old when he won a Primetime Emmy in 1973 for That Certain Summer.Roxana Zal remains the youngest acting winner overall. She was 14 when she won an Emmy in 1984 for Something About Amelia. Bindi Irwin is the youngest person to have won a Daytime Emmy, when she was nine.Getty ImagesErin Doherty said making Adolescence had been "the definition of a team effort"'Any dream is possible' - Stephen GrahamAdolescence was widely praised by critics and viewers for its exploration of the impact of smartphones and social media on teenagers.In his acceptance speech for leading actor, Graham said: "This kind of thing normally doesn't happen to a kid like me, I'm just a mixed-race kid from a block of flats in a place called Kirkby."So for me to be here today in front of my peers and be acknowledged by you is the most humbling thing I could ever imagine in my life, and it shows you that any dream is possible."Doherty, who played a child psychologist who works with Jamie, won best supporting actress in a limited series. The British actress joked she would be "banging on about Owen Cooper and Adolescence for the rest of my life"."I'd love to mention every single person involved in making this show because it was the definition of a team effort," she added.Getty ImagesStephen Graham won best lead actor in a limited series, as well as a prize for co-writing the dramaAccepting the prize for outstanding writing earlier in the night, Graham said: "We never expected our little programme to have such a big impact, but we're really grateful that it did."His co-writer Jack Thorne paid tribute to the show's young cast, telling them: "You are the flame that proved young people are going to be OK."Earlier in the weekend, actor Jake Gyllenhaal surprised Cooper during an interview, after the young actor previously described him as his hero. In a clip that went viral on social media, Gyllenhaal gave Cooper the "lucky duck" which had originally been given to him when he was nominated for an Oscar.Watch: Owen Cooper's co-stars on his Adolescence audition and being in the spotlight so young (April 2025)The Studio breaks comedy recordOther winners at the Emmys included Hollywood satire The Studio, which won several of the major comedy categories, including best actor for its star and creator Seth Rogen.The actor joked: "I couldn't wrap my head around this happening, so I've prepared nothing. "I never won anything in my life. When I was a kid I bought a used bowling trophy at an estate sale. This is really just so lovely."The Studio broke the record for the most wins by a comedy for a single series, winning 13 awards in total including those it took home at last weekend's Creative Arts Emmys.Hacks and The Pitt stars triumphGetty ImagesThe Pitt won best drama series, with Katherine LaNasa and Noah Wyle winning individual acting prizesJean Smart repeated her win for best leading actress in a comedy for her performance in the satirical Hacks.Her co-star Hannah Einbinder also won an acting prize for the first time, taking home best supporting actress in a comedy.Einbinder thanked Smart and the show's crew for "changing my life in every conceivable way". On a night were several celebrities made direct or indirect references to the Israel-Gaza war, Einbinder finished her speech by saying: "Free Palestine."Medical drama The Pitt won the prestigious best drama series award, while its stars Katherine LaNasa and Noah Wyle were recognised with individual acting prizes.But there were also wins in the drama categories for sci-fi thriller Severance, with acting prizes awarded to Britt Lower and Tramell Tillman, who became the first black winner of best supporting actor in a drama.Getty ImagesThere were acting prizes in the drama categories for Severance stars Tramell Tillman (pictured) and Britt LowerTillman dedicated his award to his mother, who he said was his "first acting coach"."Mama, you were there for me when no-one else was, and no-one else would show up," he said. "Your love and kindness stays with me, and this is for you."Departing Colbert's job pleaGetty ImagesStephen Colbert brought his old headshot as he said he was looking for work after the cancellation of The Late ShowMeanwhile, Stephen Colbert won outstanding talk series, weeks after it was announced his show was being cancelled.His win came after a recent campaign, led by some of his fellow nominees such as Jimmy Kimmel, which encouraged Academy voters to support Colbert in the category this year."Thank you for this honour," Colbert said. "I want to thank CBS for giving us to be art of the privilege of late-night tradition, which I hope continues long after we've stopped doing this show."Colbert also received a standing ovation while presenting an award earlier in the night. He joked: "While I have your attention, is anyone hiring? Because I've got 200 very well qualified candidates here with me tonight, who will be available in June."Charity boostElsewhere, the US version of The Traitors won outstanding reality competition programme.Its host Alan Cumming paid tribute to the show's "crew in Scotland who had to understand the concept that the Scottish summer is sometimes oxymoronic".He added: "It's a difficult time that we live in but it's so great that our show brings a little bit of joy into this life."Other winners included Last Week Tonight with John Oliver, which took home two prizes including best scripted variety series; Star Wars spin-off Andor, which won best drama writing; and Slow Horses, which won a directing prize.The Emmys ceremony unusually ran broadly on schedule, ending only a few minutes late.That was party due to a clever policy employed by host Nate Bargatze, who started the night by saying a $100,000 donation he was making to charity would be reduced for every speech that went over the time limit, while every short speech would add more money to the fund.But despite the amount of money going up and down throughout the night, the ceremony ended with him and broadcast network CBS announcing they would, in fact, donate $250,000 in total to US charity The Boys & Girls Clubs of America.BBC News used AI to help write the summary at the top of this article.
