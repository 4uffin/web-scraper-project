Authorities said a sniper fired toward the building from a nearby rooftop in the early morning attack.Detainees were being unloaded from a van when the shooting occurred and were being restrained for transport, per proper procedure, officials said. There were 10 detainees in the van, coming from a local jail, an ICE official told ABC News.A still from footage showing a shooting at the Dallas Immigration and Customs Enforcement field office on Sept. 24, 2025.Obtained by ABC NewsDallas ICE shooter was targeting agents, not detainees: Officials"Heroic" officers worked to take the detainees to safety while under fire, Nancy Larson, acting U.S. attorney for the Northern District of Texas, said at a news conference on Thursday.In the video, armed agents could be seen carrying victims away.In footage from inside the building, the detainees could be seen running while handcuffed and their legs shackled, some falling to the ground amid the rush to get away from the gunfire.Authorities said the suspected shooter -- 29-year-old Joshua Jahn -- "fired indiscriminately" at the ICE building and van with a bolt-action rifle.
