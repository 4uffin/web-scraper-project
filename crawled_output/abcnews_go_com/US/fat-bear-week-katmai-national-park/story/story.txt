Carter/NPSThe beefiest of all bruins are about to vie for the crown to become this year's heavyweight champion, as the 11th-annual Fat Bear Week -- a sensation that captures the attention of millions across the globe -- is set to begin.Fat Bear Week, which started in 2014, is an annual competition where brown bears from Alaska's Katmai National Park face off in head-to-head matchups -- similar to a March Madness bracket -- with people around the world voting for who they believe is the fattest of the bunch, the National Park Service said in a press release."Are you ready for #FatBearWeek, where Katmai’s beefiest bruins flaunt their fluffvying for glory as if they're the wobbliest, blue-ribbon-winning Jell-O mold at a Midwest state fair?"480 Otis walks across Brooks River in Bristol Bay, Alaska, Sept. 20, 2023. 480 Otis is estimated to be 27 years old and is a four time Fat Bear Week Champion.The Washington Post via Getty ImagesThis year's bracket will be revealed on Monday at 7 p.m. ET, according to the competition's website. People can vote on the competition's website from Sept. 23 through Sept. 30, the NPS said."Fat Bear Week enables people from around the world to work to actively engage in learning about bears while cheering for their favorite competitor," Katmai Park Superintendent Mark Sturm said in a statement.The annual competition, which is a single-elimination tournament, celebrates these rotund creatures who have successfully scarfed down enough salmon to "endure winter hibernation," the NPS said.Could a government shutdown affect Fat Bear Week?The bears, who live in the Brooks River area of the national park, can gain a few hundred pounds in fat before they retire for the winter, the NPS said.Popular ReadsTom Holland suffers concussion while filming 'Spider-Man: Brand New Day'Sep 22, 12:33 PM21-year-old man found dead after going missing in Great Smoky Mountains: NPSSep 22, 10:43 AM3-year-old, 6-year-old die in hot car outside their home, mom arrestedSep 22, 12:00 PMFrom late June through September, adult male bears in this area typically weigh anywhere from 700 to 900 pounds, but some can reach up to 1,200 pounds.
