The FBI said late Wednesday morning that Henson had been apprehended."We look at this as an act of terror against the FBI," Giordano said on Wednesday. "This was a targeted attack on this building."A vehicle sits outside the FBI Pittsburgh building after a suspect allegedly rammed the fence and fled the scene, Sept. 17, 2025.WTAEThere was "some vulgarity" scrawled on the side of the vehicle, the FBI said. The full details of what was written were not immediately clear, Giordano said, adding there appeared to be a reference to suicide.He said the suspect fled the scene and it was believed that he may be dangerous. It was not immediately clear if Henson was armed, the FBI said.Officials said no one was injured in the ramming.Henson is the registered owner of the vehicle, Giordano said. The FBI compared surveillance video with Henson’s drivers license photo to further identify him, Giordano said, adding that he also had been identified as a former military member.
