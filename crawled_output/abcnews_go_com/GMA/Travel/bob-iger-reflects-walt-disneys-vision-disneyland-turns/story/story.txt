"I felt that there should be something built where the parents and the children could have fun together."Bob Iger, CEO of The Walt Disney Co., marked the park's 70th anniversary in July by reflecting on the original vision of Disney.The Walt Disney Co. CEO Bob Iger speaks about the 70th anniversary of Disneyland in an interview that aired July 17, 2025, on "Good Morning America."ABC"He wanted people to 'leave their daily frets behind,' as he said ... and come experience, you know, the wonder of fantasy and adventure," Iger told ABC News' Will Reeve in an interview that aired July 17 on "Good Morning America.""He had very specific ideas in mind in terms of how people could enjoy this place," Iger said of Disney.
