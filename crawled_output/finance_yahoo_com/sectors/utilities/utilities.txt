Analyst Report: Public Serv Enterprise Grp Inc
Public Service Energy Group is an electric and natural gas company with generation, transmission, and distribution via its regulated subsidiary. The company also has merchant nuclear generation and energy trading through its PSEG Power operations. As of the beginning of 2025, the company's utility, PSE&G, has about 2.4 million electric and 1.9 million gas customers in New Jersey. Almost 60% of electric customers and 38% of gas customers are commercial. PSE&G Long Island provides electric transmission and distribution to 1.1 million customers. The company also wholly owns PSEG Energy Holding LLC, and PSEG Services Corp.
The company owns and operates 3 nuclear generating facilities in New Jersey and has 50% interest in two facilities in Pennsylvania. As reported in 2024, the company's generation mix is about 45% natural gas, 33% nuclear, 15% coal, and 6% renewable sources, of which 3.6% is from wind generation. Revenues in 2024 were about $10.3 billion, of which about 80% was from regulated utility operations. PEG's market cap is $41.2 billion. Rating

Analyst Report: NiSource Inc.
NiSource is one of the nation's largest natural gas distribution companies with 3.2 million customers in Indiana, Kentucky, Maryland, Ohio, Pennsylvania, and Virginia. NiSource’s electric utility transmits and distributes electricity in northern Indiana to about 500,000 customers. The regulated electric utility also owns more than 3,000 megawatts of generation capacity, including coal, natural gas, and renewable energy.

Analyst Report: Eversource Energy
Eversource Energy is a diversified holding company with subsidiaries that provide rate-regulated electric and gas distribution service to more than 4 million customers in the Northeast US. Eversource expanded its service territories with acquisitions of NStar (2012), Aquarion (2017), and Columbia Gas (2020). In 2024 Eversource exited its 50% partnership with European utility Orsted to develop 2 gigawatts of offshore wind projects in the Northeast US. The company exited most of its unregulated businesses in 2006.
