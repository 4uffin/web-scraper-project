What does Micron do?
Yahoo Finance Video
and Julie Hyman
Tue, Sep 23, 2025, 7:00 AM
In this video:
MU
Micron Technology (MU) is scheduled to share its fiscal fourth quarter earnings results after the market close on Tuesday, September 23. But what exactly does the company do? Market Catalysts host Julie Hyman discusses the history and operations of the memory chipmaker, along with everything else investors should know. Catch Yahoo Finance also explain what popular AI-powered companies Oracle (ORCL), Opendoor Technologies (OPEN), and Palantir Technologies (PLTR) do. To watch more expert insights and analysis on the latest market action, check out more Market Catalysts.
Video Transcript
00:00
Speaker A
Micron is set to report earnings in what marks the next test for artificial intelligence demand. But what exactly does Micron do? It's a memory chip maker and it's one of the best performing chip chip stocks this year. Founded in 1978 and headquartered in Boise, Idaho, Micron has grown into a global leader in DRAM. The company's growth is being fueled by high bandwidth memory, also known as HBM, that consists of bundles of DRAM chips which are closely connected to processors from Nvidia and vital for AI computing.
00:32
Speaker A
Now, what's DRAM? Well, that stands for dynamic random access memory. It's a specific type of RAM or random access memory. All computers have RAM, DRAM is one kind that we see in modern desktops and laptops. Its main competitors in that DRAM segment, Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix, both of them are based in South Korea. Micron plans to expand in the US, announcing in June it will spend about $200 billion on US manufacturing and research and development. The R&D spend along with a strategic partnerships have positioned it as a key player in the semiconductor space.
