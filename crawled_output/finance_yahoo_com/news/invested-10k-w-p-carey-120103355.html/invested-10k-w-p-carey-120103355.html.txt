If You Invested $10K In W. P. Carey Stock 10 Years Ago, How Much Would You Have Now?
David Kirakosyan
Tue, Sep 16, 2025, 7:01 AM 3 min read
In this article:
WPC
Benzinga and Yahoo Finance LLC may earn commission or revenue on some items through the links below. W. P. Carey Inc. (NYSE:WPC) is a real estate investment trust that provides capital solutions through sale-leaseback and build-to-suit financing for companies. It is set to report its Q3 2025 earnings on Oct. 28. Wall Street analysts expect the company to post EPS of $0.86, down from $1.18 in the prior-year period. According to Benzinga Pro, quarterly revenue is expected to reach $422.78 million, up from $394.77 million a year earlier.

If You Bought W. P. Carey Stock 10 Years Ago The company's stock traded at approximately $55.87 per share 10 years ago. If you had invested $10,000, you could have bought roughly 179 shares. Currently, shares trade at $68.45, meaning your investment's value could have grown to $12,252 from stock price appreciation alone. However, W. P. Carey also paid dividends during these 10 years.
W. P. Carey's dividend yield is currently 5.26%. Over the last 10 years, it has paid about $41.20 in dividends per share, which means you could have made $7,374 from dividends alone. Summing up $12,252 and $7,374, we end up with the final value of your investment, which is $19,626. This is how much you could have made if you had invested $10,000 in W. P. Carey stock 10 years ago. This means a total return of 96.26%. However, this figure is significantly less than the S&P 500 total return for the same period, which was 302%. What Could The Next 10 Years Bring? W. P. Carey has a consensus rating of "Equal Weight" and a price target of $87.18 based on the ratings of 19 analysts. The price target implies more than 27% potential upside from the current stock price. The company on July 29 announced its Q2 2025 earnings, posting FFO of $1.28, compared to the consensus estimate of $1.23, and revenues of $428.40 million, compared to the consensus of $408.69 million, as reported by Benzinga.
