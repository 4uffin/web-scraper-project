September 10, 2025, 3:47 PM4:05Screengrab of Patricia Arquette in trailer for "Murdaugh: Death in the Family."HuluA new limited scripted series based on the real-life Murdaugh family saga is coming to Hulu this fall. Hulu released the trailer for the upcoming series, "Murdaugh: Death in the Family," on Wednesday, which follows the Murdaughs, a prominent legal family with deep roots in South Carolina's Lowcountry. At the center of the new series is Alex Murdaugh, portrayed by Jason Clarke, who in real life was convicted in 2023 for the June 2021 murders of his 52 year-old wife Maggie Murdaugh and 22-year-old son Paul Murdaugh. How Alex Murdaugh's web of scandal came crashing downSee the trailer below.A synopsis for the series, which also stars Patricia Arquette as Maggie Murdaugh, states that Maggie and Alex's lavish life comes to a halt when their son Paul is involved in a deadly boat crash and they are "faced with a test unlike any they've ever encountered.""As details come to light and new challenges emerge, the family's connections to several mysterious deaths raise questions which threaten everything Maggie and Alex hold dear," the synopsis continues. Screengrab of Patricia Arquette in trailer for "Murdaugh: Death in the Family."HuluThe new limited series is inspired by Mandy Matney's popular "Murdaugh Murders Podcast," which investigated the 2021 double homicide of Paul and Maggie Murdaugh and the criminal investigations surrounding the Murdaugh family, which include unsolved deaths, insurance fraud, drugs and murder. Brittany Snow portrays Matney in the series. Read more about the Murdaugh family and timeline of key events here.Alex Murdaugh found guilty in murder of wife, sonIn May, Arquette spoke to Variety about her upcoming new role and
