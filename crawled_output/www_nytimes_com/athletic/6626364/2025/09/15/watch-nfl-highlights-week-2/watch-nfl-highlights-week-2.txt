An overtime victory by the Dallas Cowboys over the New York Giants that spoiled a throwback TD heave by Russell Wilson. The Detroit Lions dominated the Chicago Bears as first-year head coach Ben Johnson was on the losing side against his former team.AdvertisementCheck out all of the must-see moments from Sunday afternoon:Cowboys win OT thriller vs. GiantsBrandon Aubrey drilled a 46-yard field goal in overtime after booting a 64-yarder as time expired in regulation as the Cowboys won 40-37.Eagles remain NFL’s team to beatThe defending champs shut down Patrick Mahomes and the Chiefs, pulling away late for a 20-17 victory in a defensive struggle.Falcons shine against sputtering VikingsNewly signed Parker Romo made all five of his field-goal attempts, including a 54-yarder, to score the majority of Atlanta’s points as J.J. McCarthy and Minnesota were unable to get going all game.Lions put on a clinic against BearsJared Goff threw for 334 yards and matched a career high with five touchdown passes as Detroit dominated Chicago 52-21.Colts connect on second chanceAfter missing a 60-yard try where the Broncos were flagged for a 15-yard penalty, Spencer Shrader hit a 45-yard field goal as time expired, lifting the Colts to a 29-28 comeback win.Patriots’ wild victory over DolphinsDrake Maye passed for 230 yards and two touchdowns, and added 31 rushing yards and another score on the ground, as the Patriots took advantage of Miami’s defensive struggles in a wild 33-27 victory.Steelers’ gaffe gives Seahawks winSeattle picked up its first win of the season, capitalizing on Pittsburgh’s critical fourth-quarter muffed kick return to earn a 31-17 win.Dominant Bills defense shuts down JetsJames Cook rushed for two touchdowns, Matt Prater kicked three field goals and Buffalo throttled New York 30-10 despite Josh Allen throwing for only 148 yards.Mac Jones leads 49ers to 2-0 startStarting in place of an injured Brock Purdy, Mac Jones threw three touchdown passes to lead a short-handed 49ers team to a 26-21 victory against the Saints.Arizona wins despite Bryce Young’s career dayCalais Campbell’s sack of Bryce Young on fourth down brought a sudden end to a furious comeback by the Panthers, preserving a 27-22 victory for the Cardinals.Ravens get redemption against BrownsBaltimore’s defense played like it had something to prove, bouncing back from last week’s collapse and helping secure a win over Cleveland and turning a 10-3 first-half struggle into a 41-17 blowout.Joe Burrow’s toe injury spoils Bengals’ winJoe Burrow left Sunday’s 31-27 win against the Jacksonville Jaguars in the second quarter and was ruled out with a toe injury.Cam Ward shows flashes, but LA’s defense dominatesMatthew Stafford passed for 298 yards and two touchdowns as the Rams pulled away late to beat the Titans, 33-19, and overcome some positive moments from Tennessee’s rookie quarterback.(Photo of Brandon Aubrey: Sam Hodde / Getty Images)
