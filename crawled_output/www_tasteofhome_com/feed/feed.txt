Get ready to meet your new game-day obsession! These white chicken chili nachos are fully loaded and capture everything you love about the cozy, slow-simmered flavor of white chicken chili, but in a convenient nacho form. The chili is piled high on crispy tortilla chips, smothered in melty cheese and finished with all the fixings: fresh jalapenos, sour cream and cilantro. It's comfort food with a crunch—bold, messy and made to share. —Elise Jesse, Cincinnati, Ohio

White Cheddar vs. Cheddar: What’s the Difference?
https://www.tasteofhome.com/article/white-cheddar-vs-cheddar-difference/
Hayley Schueneman
Fri, 19 Sep 2025 18:54:38 +0000
News
Trends
https://www.tasteofhome.com/?p=2184560
<p>It's quite simple, really.</p>
<p>The post <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/article/white-cheddar-vs-cheddar-difference/">White Cheddar vs. Cheddar: What&#8217;s the Difference?</a> appeared first on <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com">Taste of Home</a>.</p>
<p>One of my absolute favorite comfort foods is macaroni and cheese. When I&rsquo;m feeling ambitious, I&rsquo;ll make it from scratch, using a roux as the base for a smooth and creamy <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/cheddar-cheese-sauce/" data-name="www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/cheddar-cheese-sauce/" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links">cheese sauce</a>. But if I&rsquo;m feeling under the weather or a little blue, I don&rsquo;t always have the energy for that kind of kitchen undertaking.</p><p>That&rsquo;s when I reach for my favorite boxed mac and cheese: Annie&rsquo;s Shells and White Cheddar. That distinctive purple box always puts me in a good mood. Recently, I couldn&rsquo;t help but wonder: Does <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/white-cheddar-mac-cheese/" data-name="www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/white-cheddar-mac-cheese/" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links">white cheddar</a> actually <em>taste</em> different than yellow cheddar? I realized that I didn&rsquo;t have an answer.</p><p>So I decided to go on a cheddar quest, straight to the source. I visited the <a href="https://www.tillamook.com/visit-us/creamery" data-name="www.tillamook.com/visit-us/creamery" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Tillamook Creamery</a> in Tillamook, Oregon, as well as one of their dairy farms. What can I say, I&rsquo;m <em>very</em> thorough!</p><h2>Cheddar vs. white cheddar: What&rsquo;s the difference?</h2><p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="alignnone wp-image-2184882 size-large" src="https://www.tasteofhome.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/White-Cheddar-vs-Cheddar_IMG_9681_Hayley-Schueneman-for-taste-of-home.jpg?fit=700%2C1024" alt="" width="700" height="1024" data-recalc-dims="1" title="White Cheddar Vs Cheddar Img 9681 Hayley Schueneman For Taste Of Home" data-has-syndication-rights="1" data-portal-copyright="HAYLEY SCHUENEMAN FOR TASTE OF HOME"></p><p>If you want to be cheeky and say, &ldquo;Well, they&rsquo;re different colors,&rdquo; you&rsquo;re actually about halfway to the truth. As Jill Allen, Director of Product Excellence at Tillamook, pointed out to me, all cheddar is naturally white. That&rsquo;s because cheese is made from milk, which is white. But it&rsquo;s not necessarily <em>uniformly</em> white.</p><p>From pale, buttery cream shades with just a tinge of yellow to bright, almost cool-toned white shades, milk can be different colors from the white spectrum. Jill explained that there are several factors can that influence the color of a cow&rsquo;s milk, including the time of year, the temperature and the cow&rsquo;s diet. Even from cow to cow there can be a lot of variation.</p><p>In order to make blocks of cheddar look more uniform, a coloring agent called annatto is added to the milk as it begins its journey to becoming cheese.</p><h2>What is annatto?</h2><p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="alignnone wp-image-2184883 size-large" src="https://www.tasteofhome.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/White-Cheddar-vs-Cheddar_IMG_9720_Hayley-Schueneman-for-taste-of-home.jpg?fit=700%2C1024" alt="" width="700" height="1024" data-recalc-dims="1" title="White Cheddar Vs Cheddar Img 9720 Hayley Schueneman For Taste Of Home" data-has-syndication-rights="1" data-portal-copyright="HAYLEY SCHUENEMAN FOR TASTE OF HOME"></p><p>Annatto is made from the seeds of the achiote tree, which is indigenous to Central and South America, Mexico, and the Caribbean. The annatto seeds can be ground into a powder and used as an edible dye. It&rsquo;s in many products, from smoked fish to lipstick to, of course, cheese.</p><p>I first learned about annatto when I was working at Whole Foods. It was one of the natural dyes that the bakery department used to give frosting an orange tint. Costco and Ethica Wines (the product&rsquo;s importer) are urging members to check their bottles and handle them with care.</p><h2>What stores sold the wine?</h2><p data-start="1014" data-end="1222">This recall only affects Kirkland Signature Prosecco Valdobbiadene, which is a Costco-exclusive brand. If you purchased Prosecco from Costco in any of the following states, it could be part of the recall: Iowa, Illinois, Indiana, Kentucky, Michigan, Minnesota, Missouri, North Dakota, Nebraska, Ohio, South Dakota and Wisconsin.</p><h2>How do I know if I have a recalled wine?</h2><p>Here&rsquo;s what to look for:</p><p><strong>Kirkland Signature Prosecco Valdobbiadene</strong></p><ul><li>Item number on the label: 1879870</li><li>Purchase date: Between April 25, 2025 and August 26, 2025</li><li>Costco location you purchased from was in one of the states listed above</li></ul><p>If all of those match, your wine bottle is affected.</p><h2>What should I do next?</h2><p data-start="1834" data-end="2031">Do not try to open the bottle, and do not bring it back into the store. To safely dispose of it, wrap it in paper towels, put it in a plastic bag, and place it in the trash. Then, bring the recall notice (or just your receipt) to your local Costco for a full refund.</p><p data-start="1834" data-end="2031">If you have questions, you can contact Ethica Wines at customercare@ethicawines.com or by calling 786-810-7132 Monday through Friday between 8 a.m. and 6 p.m. EST.</p><p>The post <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/article/costco-wine-recall-september-2025/">Costco Wine Recall: Bottles of Prosecco Are at Risk of Shattering</a> appeared first on <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com">Taste of Home</a>.</p>

I Made the Viral TikTok Pickled Avocados and Wanted to Put Them on Everything
https://www.tasteofhome.com/article/pickled-avocados/
https://www.tasteofhome.com/article/pickled-avocados/#respond
Amanda Blum
Mon, 15 Sep 2025 22:57:28 +0000
News
Trends
Viral Recipes
https://www.tasteofhome.com/?p=2177113
<p>Pickled avocados are a brilliant snack that can make unripe avocado edible and keep it fresh for a week.</p>
<p>The post <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/article/pickled-avocados/">I Made the Viral TikTok Pickled Avocados and Wanted to Put Them on Everything</a> appeared first on <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com">Taste of Home</a>.</p>
<p>My friends have long joked that in the event of a zombie apocalypse, my wall of pickled vegetables could sustain us for a few weeks of good eating. And despite having pickled my fair share of fruits&mdash;strawberries and cherry tomatoes being a couple favorites&mdash;I hadn&rsquo;t yet heard of pickled avocados.</p><p>Though there are plenty of <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/collection/pickled-recipes/" data-name="www.tasteofhome.com/collection/pickled-recipes/" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links" rel="noopener noreferrer">foods you can pickle</a>, avocados weren&rsquo;t on my radar until this summer, when videos of people raving about them took over my TikTok. Finally, a way to use up extremely unripe avocados instead of sitting around waiting for them to soften! Although the recipe for the <a href="https://www.instagram.com/reel/DJz39R4zHzw/?igsh=ZWVuOHF3cDE1cGJn" data-name="www.instagram.com/reel/DJz39R4zHzw/?igsh=ZWVuOHF3cDE1cGJn" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow">viral TikTok version</a> was simple enough, I stuck to my own tried-and-true pickling brine recipe. The process is quick; it took less than 10 minutes to make two jars of avocado pickles.</p><h2>How ripe should the avocados be?</h2><p>Your best chance for success is to use very unripe avocados. Look for fruit with skin that is bright green. When you gently squeeze the avocado, there should be no give at all. I found more success with small to medium avocados, than with larger versions. Three small to medium avocados filled two pint jars (each jar fit one and a half avocados).</p><h2>How to Pickle Avocados</h2><h3>Ingredients</h3><ul><li>3 unripe avocados</li><li>1 cup apple cider vinegar</li><li>1 cup water</li><li>2 tablespoons salt</li><li>2 tablespoons honey</li><li>2 cloves garlic</li><li>2 tablespoons lemon juice</li><li>2 teaspoons coriander seeds</li><li>2 teaspoons mustard seeds</li><li>2 teaspoons peppercorns</li><li>1 jalapeno, optional</li></ul><h3>Directions</h3><h4>Step 1: Prep your jars</h4><p>Prepare two wide-mouth pint-size glass jars with lids. Plastic or metal lids are fine here. Thoroughly clean the jars. A dishwasher is best, but if you&rsquo;re hand-washing, rinse the containers with a bit of distilled white vinegar after washing, dump the vinegar and then, without rinsing, allow the container to dry.</p><h4>Step 2: Gather your ingredients</h4><p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-2182257" src="https://www.tasteofhome.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/I-Made-the-Viral-TikTok-Pickled-Avocados_TOHD25_Pickle-Avocados_SarahTramonte_1.jpg?fit=700%2C1024" alt="" width="700" height="1024" data-recalc-dims="1" title="I Made The Viral Tiktok Pickled Avocados Tohd25 Pickle Avocados Sarahtramonte 1" data-has-syndication-rights="1" data-portal-copyright="Sarah Tramonte for Taste of Home"></p><p>Assemble your ingredients and divide the seasonings&mdash;the garlic, seeds and chili pepper&mdash;between the two jars.</p><p><em>Editor&rsquo;s Tip:</em> How much jalapeno you add is a very personal choice, but you should always use less than you think. Even a very small amount of jalapeno is going to make the pickles quite spicy. If you don&rsquo;t know where to start, cut a sliver of the pepper to place into each container.</p><h4>Step 3: Boil the brine</h4><p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-2182256" src="https://www.tasteofhome.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/I-Made-the-Viral-TikTok-Pickled-Avocados_TOHD25_Pickle-Avocados_SarahTramonte_3.jpg?fit=700%2C1024" alt="" width="700" height="1024" data-recalc-dims="1" title="I Made The Viral Tiktok Pickled Avocados Tohd25 Pickle Avocados Sarahtramonte 3" data-has-syndication-rights="1" data-portal-copyright="Sarah Tramonte for Taste of Home"></p><p>In a saucepan over medium-high heat, combine the vinegar, water, salt and honey. Bring the mixture to a boil and stir it until the salt and honey dissolve. Turn off the heat.</p><h4>Step 4: Cut and peel the avocado</h4><p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-2182250" src="https://www.tasteofhome.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/I-Made-the-Viral-TikTok-Pickled-Avocados_TOHD25_Pickle-Avocados_SarahTramonte_2.jpg?fit=700%2C1024" alt="" width="700" height="1024" data-recalc-dims="1" title="I Made The Viral Tiktok Pickled Avocados Tohd25 Pickle Avocados Sarahtramonte 2" data-has-syndication-rights="1" data-portal-copyright="Sarah Tramonte for Taste of Home"></p><p>Cut each avocado in half around the seed, and separate them. Using your knife, carefully remove the seed of the avocado. Since the fruit is unripe, you may find this harder than usual, but you can carefully lodge the knife in the seed and then carefully twist the knife so the seed pops out.</p><p>Using a vegetable peeler, peel each half of the avocado to remove all the skin. You should be left with pale green flesh. Lay each half, cut side down, on a cutting board and slice them the long way into wedges. Each avocado half should yield seven to eight wedges.</p><h4>Step 5: Place the avocados in the jars</h4><p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-2182255" src="https://www.tasteofhome.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/I-Made-the-Viral-TikTok-Pickled-Avocados_TOHD25_Pickle-Avocados_SarahTramonte_4.jpg?fit=700%2C1024" alt="" width="700" height="1024" data-recalc-dims="1" title="I Made The Viral Tiktok Pickled Avocados Tohd25 Pickle Avocados Sarahtramonte 4" data-has-syndication-rights="1" data-portal-copyright="Sarah Tramonte for Taste of Home"></p><p>Carefully place each wedge into the container, keeping them upright. Don&rsquo;t force pieces in; try to make space for them by turning the container on its side and tapping the side. Each container should fit one and a half avocados.</p><p>Add half of the lemon juice to each container, pouring it over the avocados.</p><h4>Step 6: Pour in the brine</h4><p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-2182259" src="https://www.tasteofhome.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/I-Made-the-Viral-TikTok-Pickled-Avocados_TOHD25_Pickle-Avocados_SarahTramonte_5.jpg?fit=700%2C1024" alt="" width="700" height="1024" data-recalc-dims="1" title="I Made The Viral Tiktok Pickled Avocados Tohd25 Pickle Avocados Sarahtramonte 5" data-has-syndication-rights="1" data-portal-copyright="Sarah Tramonte for Taste of Home"></p><p>Carefully pour the hot brine over the fruit, ensuring that all the fruit is completely submerged. You should have more than enough brine to fill two pint jars, stopping 1/2 inch short of the rim.</p><h4>Step 7: Refrigerate the pickled avocados</h4><p>Allow the containers to cool to room temperature on the counter, and then place them in the refrigerator. Wait at least 12 hours before trying your pickles.</p><p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-2182261" src="https://www.tasteofhome.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/I-Made-the-Viral-TikTok-Pickled-Avocados_TOHD25_Pickle-Avocados_SarahTramonte_6.jpg?fit=700%2C1024" alt="" width="700" height="1024" data-recalc-dims="1" title="I Made The Viral Tiktok Pickled Avocados Tohd25 Pickle Avocados Sarahtramonte 6" data-has-syndication-rights="1" data-portal-copyright="Sarah Tramonte for Taste of Home"></p><h2>How to Serve Pickled Avocado</h2><p>I mostly found myself eating the pickled avocado out of the jar with a fork. I kept returning to it, surprised by how addictive it was (much like any other pickle). While you probably won&rsquo;t want to cook with the avocado, it makes a fantastic garnish.</p><p>Layering segments on top of <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/collection/top-10-grilled-fish-recipes/" data-name="www.tasteofhome.com/collection/top-10-grilled-fish-recipes/" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links" rel="noopener noreferrer">grilled fish</a> or chicken was a wonderful way to use these pickles. The vinegar and brightness was a nice complement to the char of the grill. The pickles should have enough firmness to hold together if you chop them into a medium dice, which makes an even prettier garnish or addition to salsa. Since the avocados are preserved, they will retain their color and texture&mdash;even in the salsa.</p><p>Obviously, these pickles belong in tacos. You could replace your pickled onions with avocado pickles and get the best of both worlds: an acidic break from the taco filling, and the familiar taste and texture of avocado.</p><p>They&rsquo;re still avocados, and you can treat them as such by making a <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/easy-guacamole/" data-name="www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/easy-guacamole/" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links" rel="noopener noreferrer">guacamole</a> with them. It&rsquo;ll pack a different punch than traditional guac, with a satisfying vinegary bite.</p><h2>How long will pickled avocados last?</h2><p>These are <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/easy-refrigerator-pickles/" data-name="www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/easy-refrigerator-pickles/" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links" rel="noopener noreferrer">refrigerator pickles</a>, meaning that they haven&rsquo;t undergone a real canning process. Refrigerator pickles are best eaten within 7 to 10 days to ensure they stay nice and crisp. After that point, they may still be safe, but the fruit will begin to deteriorate and become soft.</p><p>The biggest threat to refrigerator pickles is the introduction of bacteria by using your hands to eat them, or reusing a fork that you eat off of. Only dip clean utensils into the pickle jar. If you see any white fuzz on the pickles, it&rsquo;s time to toss them.</p><script async type="text/javascript" src="//tracking.skyword.com/tracker.js?contentId=281474980448640"></script><p>The post <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/article/pickled-avocados/">I Made the Viral TikTok Pickled Avocados and Wanted to Put Them on Everything</a> appeared first on <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com">Taste of Home</a>.</p>
