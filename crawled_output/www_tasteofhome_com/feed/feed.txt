<p>No, his favorite food is not moonbeam ice cream.</p>
<p>The post <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/article/benson-boone-favorite-food/">Benson Boone&#8217;s Favorite Food Is One of His Mom&#8217;s Recipes</a> appeared first on <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com">Taste of Home</a>.</p>
<p>Benson Boone is everywhere. You might know him as the guy who did a flip off of a piano at this year&rsquo;s Grammy Awards. Even if you didn&rsquo;t catch the Grammys, I absolutely guarantee that you know at least one of his songs, &ldquo;Beautiful Things.&rdquo; It won 2025 Song of The Year at the iHeartRadio Music Awards and has been the song in one out of every three TikToks or Instagram Reels you&rsquo;ve seen over the last year.</p><p>When he isn&rsquo;t releasing new music or filming new videos, he&rsquo;s probably refueling with one of his favorite foods. Since he calls out moonbeam ice cream in another one of his songs, &ldquo;Mystical Magical,&rdquo; I had to assume that ice cream would be at the top of his list. But to my surprise, he actually prefers a savory dish&mdash;and what&rsquo;s more, it&rsquo;s one of his mom&rsquo;s recipes. Aww, Benson!</p><h2>What is Benson Boone&rsquo;s favorite food?</h2><p>Benson Boone loves his mom&rsquo;s lasagna! He&rsquo;s mentioned it in a few different interviews over the years. While tasting a pavlova for a <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fGWjIVfqF9I" data-name="www.youtube.com/watch?v=fGWjIVfqF9I" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Kiwi Snacks</a> review on YouTube, he mentions that the fluffy white dessert is nearly perfect. The caveat? &ldquo;I mean, nothing can be perfect,&rdquo; he says. &ldquo;Except maybe my mom&rsquo;s lasagna.&rdquo;</p><p>I am in the same camp as Benson Boone and Garfield the Cat: I think lasagna is incredible. My mom actually makes a protein-packed plant-based version with tofu and spinach in place of ricotta cheese, and it&rsquo;s such a genius swap that I always forget that it&rsquo;s vegan. And Julianne Moore is also famous for having a really great <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/article/julianne-moore-favorite-food/" data-name="www.tasteofhome.com/article/julianne-moore-favorite-food/" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links">vegetable lasagna</a>. Her recipe, which included asparagus and zucchini, was even included in a charity cookbook.</p><p>Aside from his mom&rsquo;s lasagna, Benson Boone&rsquo;s other favorite food is probably cereal. He considers himself a bit of a <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aCObwxm-gl0" data-name="www.youtube.com/watch?v=aCObwxm-gl0" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">breakfast cereal enthusiast</a>, with his favorite being Lucky Charms. They&rsquo;re mystically magically delicious!</p><h2>Related:</h2><ul><li><a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/article/addison-rae-starbucks-order/?int_campaign=tmb_trend_recirc&amp;int_source=direct&amp;int_medium=tmb.com&amp;int_placement=single_card" data-name="www.tasteofhome.com/article/addison-rae-starbucks-order/?int_campaign=tmb_trend_recirc&amp;int_source=direct&amp;int_medium=tmb.com&amp;int_placement=single_card" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links">This is Addison Rae&rsquo;s Go-To Starbucks Order</a></li><li><a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/article/pedro-pascals-favorite-breakfast/?int_campaign=tmb_trend_recirc&amp;int_source=direct&amp;int_medium=tmb.com&amp;int_placement=single_card" data-name="www.tasteofhome.com/article/pedro-pascals-favorite-breakfast/?int_campaign=tmb_trend_recirc&amp;int_source=direct&amp;int_medium=tmb.com&amp;int_placement=single_card" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links">Pedro Pascal&rsquo;s Favorite Breakfast Is a Big Bowl of Nostalgia</a></li><li><a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/article/timothee-chalamet-breakfast/?int_campaign=tmb_trend_recirc&amp;int_source=direct&amp;int_medium=tmb.com&amp;int_placement=single_card" data-name="www.tasteofhome.com/article/timothee-chalamet-breakfast/?int_campaign=tmb_trend_recirc&amp;int_source=direct&amp;int_medium=tmb.com&amp;int_placement=single_card" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links">This Is What Timoth&eacute;e Chalamet Eats for Breakfast Every Day</a></li></ul><p>The post <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/article/benson-boone-favorite-food/">Benson Boone&#8217;s Favorite Food Is One of His Mom&#8217;s Recipes</a> appeared first on <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com">Taste of Home</a>.</p>

What can I say, I&rsquo;m <em>very</em> thorough!</p><h2>Cheddar vs. white cheddar: What&rsquo;s the difference?</h2><p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="alignnone wp-image-2184882 size-large" src="https://www.tasteofhome.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/White-Cheddar-vs-Cheddar_IMG_9681_Hayley-Schueneman-for-taste-of-home.jpg?fit=680%2C995" alt="" width="700" height="1024" data-recalc-dims="1" title="White Cheddar Vs Cheddar Img 9681 Hayley Schueneman For Taste Of Home" data-has-syndication-rights="1" data-portal-copyright="HAYLEY SCHUENEMAN FOR TASTE OF HOME"></p><p>If you want to be cheeky and say, &ldquo;Well, they&rsquo;re different colors,&rdquo; you&rsquo;re actually about halfway to the truth. As Jill Allen, Director of Product Excellence at Tillamook, pointed out to me, all cheddar is naturally white. That&rsquo;s because cheese is made from milk, which is white. <p>Spicy food tolerance can definitely improve over time. Young Chang of A-Sha Foods shares his best tips for handling spicy food like a pro.</p>
<p>The post <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/article/spicy-food-tolerance-tips/">4 Ways to Increase Your Tolerance of Spicy Foods</a> appeared first on <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com">Taste of Home</a>.</p>
<p>As the saying goes, variety is the spice of life. It&rsquo;s also true that spice adds variety to life and brings so much vibrant flavor to dishes. While I consider myself an open-minded cook and eater, there is one place where I feel a little behind. Unfortunately, my spicy food tolerance is pretty low, and I really want to work on improving it.</p><p>I&rsquo;ve definitely gotten a <em>little</em> better at tolerating spice over time. But I want to dive headfirst into Dan Dan noodles or Korean barbecue chicken without being completely overwhelmed! Since I know I&rsquo;m not alone in this conundrum, I decided to ask a spicy food expert for some pro tips on how to incorporate spicier dishes into my life.</p><p>I spoke with Young Chang, the founder and CEO of A-Sha Foods. Start with the basics</h3><p><b>Taste of Home:</b> What kinds of spicy foods are good for beginners, aka people who don&rsquo;t have much spice tolerance but want to expand their palate?</p><p><strong>Young Chang:</strong> Start with foods that balance flavor and heat, rather than going for intensity right away. Look for dishes that pair chile with garlic, sesame or a touch of sweetness&mdash;something that gives a warm tingle rather than a full burn. Thai sweet chili sauce, gochujang or <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/collection/best-chili-crisp/" data-name="www.tasteofhome.com/collection/best-chili-crisp/" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links">chili crisp</a> are great starter condiments. Noodle dishes that layer spice with umami are also an approachable intro.</p><h3>2. Turn down the heat</h3><p><strong>TOH: </strong>If you become overwhelmed by spicy food, what&rsquo;s the best way to alleviate that sensation?</p><p><strong>YC:</strong> First rule: don&rsquo;t reach for water! Water can actually spread the spice oils around your mouth and make it worse. Instead, go for dairy-based drinks (like milk or yogurt), or even bread or rice to help absorb and neutralize the heat. A spoonful of honey or sugar can also help tame the burn.</p><h3>3. Make it a habit</h3><p><strong>TOH:</strong> We&rsquo;ve all seen people like <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yJpUBIA3ih0" data-name="www.youtube.com/watch?v=yJpUBIA3ih0" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Demi Moore eat super spicy food on Hot Ones</a> and not even flinch. Are some people just not affected by spice?</p><p><strong>YC:</strong> Some people do seem to have a naturally higher spice tolerance, whether from genetics, exposure or practice. Over time, your brain can become desensitized to the pain signal triggered by capsaicin (the active compound in chile peppers). It&rsquo;s a real phenomenon, and a spicy badge of honor! In fact, A-Sha&rsquo;s spice-loving fans inspired us to partner with Hot Ones to create a fiery lineup of increasingly spicy and flavorful ramen noodle kits that turn up the heat with every bite. If you&rsquo;re interested in trying them out, you can purchase A-Sha x Hot Ones Ramen Cups and Bowls at Walmart and Target.</p><p><strong>TOH:</strong> Is it possible to get used to spiciness over time?</p><p><strong>YC:</strong> Absolutely! Like any other flavor, your spice tolerance can build with exposure. Start slow, eat spicy foods more regularly, and give your taste buds time to adapt. A great approach is to layer a little chili oil or hot sauce into a dish, then gradually add more over time.</p><h3>4. Balance your plate</h3><p><b>TOH:</b> What kinds of meals or dishes have a good balance of flavors that incorporate spice, but don&rsquo;t overpower it?</p><p><strong>YC:</strong> Dishes that balance sweet, salty, sour and spicy are ideal. Examples include <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/thai-chicken-peanut-noodles:/" data-name="www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/thai-chicken-peanut-noodles:/" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links">Thai noodle dishes</a>, Sichuan-style Dan Dan noodles and Korean <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/kimchi-fried-rice/" data-name="www.tasteofhome.com/recipes/kimchi-fried-rice/" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links">kimchi fried rice</a>. These meals use spice to complement, not dominate, other flavor notes. That&rsquo;s something we strive for with A-Sha&rsquo;s spicy lineup: bold but balanced.</p><p><strong>TOH: </strong>Any final thoughts on spice?</p><p><strong>YC: </strong>Spice is meant to elevate, not punish. <p data-start="195" data-end="502">If you picked up a bottle of Kirkland Signature Prosecco Valdobbiadene at Costco earlier this year, then you&rsquo;ll want to double-check your stash. Costco has issued a recall for certain bottles due to a safety issue that could cause them to break unexpectedly&mdash;even if they&rsquo;re just sitting unopened on your wine rack. Here&rsquo;s what you need to know.</p><h2>What caused the recall?</h2><p>According to a <a href="https://cdn.bfldr.com/U447IH35/as/3q6998t8pfg5hhjpkcpgvbc/FinalKSProseccoValdoMemberLetter" data-name="cdn.bfldr.com/U447IH35/as/3q6998t8pfg5hhjpkcpgvbc/FinalKSProseccoValdoMemberLetter" data-module="content engagement" data-position="embedded links" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">notice sent to Costco members</a>, some bottles of the Prosecco may spontaneously shatter&mdash;even if they haven&rsquo;t been touched. That&rsquo;s a serious safety risk, as flying glass could cause injury. Costco and Ethica Wines (the product&rsquo;s importer) are urging members to check their bottles and handle them with care.</p><h2>What stores sold the wine?</h2><p data-start="1014" data-end="1222">This recall only affects Kirkland Signature Prosecco Valdobbiadene, which is a Costco-exclusive brand. If you purchased Prosecco from Costco in any of the following states, it could be part of the recall: Iowa, Illinois, Indiana, Kentucky, Michigan, Minnesota, Missouri, North Dakota, Nebraska, Ohio, South Dakota and Wisconsin.</p><h2>How do I know if I have a recalled wine?</h2><p>Here&rsquo;s what to look for:</p><p><strong>Kirkland Signature Prosecco Valdobbiadene</strong></p><ul><li>Item number on the label: 1879870</li><li>Purchase date: Between April 25, 2025 and August 26, 2025</li><li>Costco location you purchased from was in one of the states listed above</li></ul><p>If all of those match, your wine bottle is affected.</p><h2>What should I do next?</h2><p data-start="1834" data-end="2031">Do not try to open the bottle, and do not bring it back into the store. To safely dispose of it, wrap it in paper towels, put it in a plastic bag, and place it in the trash. Then, bring the recall notice (or just your receipt) to your local Costco for a full refund.</p><p data-start="1834" data-end="2031">If you have questions, you can contact Ethica Wines at customercare@ethicawines.com or by calling 786-810-7132 Monday through Friday between 8 a.m. and 6 p.m. EST.</p><p>The post <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com/article/costco-wine-recall-september-2025/">Costco Wine Recall: Bottles of Prosecco Are at Risk of Shattering</a> appeared first on <a href="https://www.tasteofhome.com">Taste of Home</a>.</p>
