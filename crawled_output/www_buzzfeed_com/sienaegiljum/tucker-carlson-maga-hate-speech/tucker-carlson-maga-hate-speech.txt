It's been a truly wild week, my friends. And although I wish I could say that nothing surprises me anymore, that would be a lie.
NBC
In the wake of the fatal shooting of political activist Charlie Kirk last week, some politicians on the right have been keen to deliver consequences to people they determine are "celebrating" Kirk's death. Here's a tweet from Rep. Nancy Mace applauding the firing of three Clemson University employees who she said were "cheering for assassinations."
RepNancyMace / ClemsonUniv / Via x.com
Just yesterday, Jimmy Kimmel's late-night show was pulled from the air due to threats from the FCC over his comments about the potential political leanings of the shooter. And among the voices sounding the alarm on the dissolution of the right to free speech is conservative commentator Tucker Carlson.
Carlson appeared with a strong message on the subject earlier this week on his podcast, The Tucker Carlson Show. A particular clip from the show has since gone viral on X.
"You hope that Charlie Kirk's death won't be used by a group we now call bad actors to create a society that was the opposite of the one he worked to build," he says in the clip. "You hope that."
The Tucker Carlson Show / Via x.com

"You hope that a year from now, the turmoil we're seeing in the aftermath of his murder won't be leveraged to bring hate speech laws to this country."
The Tucker Carlson Show / Via x.com
"And trust me, if it is, if that does happen, there is never a more justified moment for civil disobedience than that. And there never will be," Carlson says.
The Tucker Carlson Show / Via x.com
"Because if they can tell you what to say, they're telling you what to think, there is nothing they can't do to you, because they don't consider you human. They don't believe you have a soul."
The Tucker Carlson Show / Via x.com
"A human being with a soul, a free man, has a right to say what he believes," he says at the end of the clip. "Not to hurt other people, but to express his views."
The Tucker Carlson Show / Via x.com
