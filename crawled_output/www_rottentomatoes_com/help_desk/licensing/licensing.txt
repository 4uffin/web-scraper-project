Our Trademarks and Logos
Below are our guidelines for using any of our trademarks or logos.
Rotten Tomatoes® Logo

Rotten Tomatoes scores and reviews must link back to the corresponding movie pages on https://www.rottentomatoes.com.
The Fresh Tomato or Hot Popcorn Logo may only be displayed in connection with a film or television show
if it received a score of 60% or higher. When using either logo, please display it to the left of the title's score.
The Rotten Splat or Stale Popcorn Logo may only be displayed in connection with a film or television show
if it received a score of 59% or lower. When using either logo, please display it to the left of the title's score.
The Fresh Tomato, Rotten Splat, Hot Popcorn, and Stale Popcorn Logos cannot be altered without prior written permission.
Certified Fresh™ Logo
Rotten Tomatoes awards the Certified Fresh™ accolade to films and TV programs that have a
Tomatometer® of 75% or higher after a set number of reviews (80 for films in wide
release, 40 for films in limited release, 20 for TV shows), including 5 reviews from Top
Critics. A film remains Certified Fresh unless its Tomatometer falls below 70%. Reserved
for the best reviewed films, the Certified Fresh accolade constitutes a seal of approval,
synonymous with quality.
Rotten Tomatoes encourages use of the Certified Fresh accolade for editorial and
marketing purposes provided the following requirements are met (in addition to our
general use guidelines):
The Certified Fresh Logo may only be used in connection with films that Rotten
Tomatoes has designated as Certified Fresh.
The Certified Fresh Logo cannot be altered without prior written permission.
The link to the Certified Fresh Logo must go to one of the following landing pages on the Rotten Tomatoes site:
The Rotten Tomatoes review page for that specific movie
The Rotten Tomatoes homepage
The Certified Fresh Movie web page(s)
Verified Hot Logo
Rotten Tomatoes awards the Verified Hot accolade to films that have a Verified
Popcornmeter™ of 90% or higher after a set number of ratings (500 for films in
wide release, 250 for films in limited release). A film remains Verified Hot unless its
Popcornmeter falls below 80%. Verified Hot is an elevated designation for the
Popcornmeter, honoring the highest verified reviewed theatrical films on the Popcornmeter.
Rotten Tomatoes encourages use of the Verified Hot accolade for editorial and marketing
purposes provided the following requirements are met (in addition to our general use
guidelines):
The Verified Hot Logo may only be used in connection with films that Rotten Tomatoes
has designated as Verified Hot.
The Verified Hot Logo cannot be altered without prior written permission.
The link to the Verified Hot Logo must go to one of the following landing pages on the Rotten Tomatoes site:
The Rotten Tomatoes review page for that specific movie
The Rotten Tomatoes homepage
The Verified Hot Movie web page(s)
