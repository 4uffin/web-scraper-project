PlayStation State Of Play September 2025: All The Biggest Announcements And GamesBy
Jason Fanelli
on September 24, 2025 at 3:02PM PDT
GameSpot may receive revenue from affiliate and advertising partnerships for sharing this content and from purchases through links.
Time for another State of Play!As Tokyo Game Show approaches, many video game publishers and developers are trying to get news out about their latest projects to as many people as possible. Today, PlayStation started TGS week with a bang, as a new State of Play livestream brought a whole bunch of news on previously announced games as well as all-new announcements. Alongside release windows and dates for heavily anticipated titles, including Saros and Wolverine, we also got a look at some new and unexpected hardware from Sony. Whether you are into action, role-playing, immersive sims, or vehicle simulators, there was something for you during this presentation. Check it all out below. Many of hero Arjun Devraj's abilities were shown off, including the projectile-absorbing Soltari Shield, as well as the Power Weapon, which sends the absorbed projectiles back at Arjun's foes. Housemarque's Saros launches March 20, 2026.Saros Release Date Confirmed With New Gameplay TrailerZero Parades for Dead SpiesNext was the latest narrative adventure from Disco Elysium studio ZA/UM, Zero Parades for Dead Spies. The brief gameplay trailer showed some of the detective protagonist's unconventional investigative techniques, along with a brief glimpse at dialogue and customization options. Zero Parades is set to launch in 2026.Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024Microsoft's next port from Xbox to PlayStation will take flight on December 8, as Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024 has booked a trip to Xbox's main competitor.Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024 Soaring To PS5Battlefield 6A new trailer for Battlefield 6 was next on the docket, focusing specifically on the game's campaign mode. Battlefield 6 launches October 10.Battlefield 6 Gets New Campaign Trailer Featuring Tons Of Explosions During State Of PlayDeus Ex RemasteredThe classic PC first-person shooter Deus Ex is making its way to PlayStation to celebrate its 25th anniversary, with new features exclusive to the remaster. It's set to launch February 5, 2026.Stealth Classic Deus Ex Gets Remastered In 2026Halloween: The GameIllfonic's next asymmetrical multiplayer horror game will take players to the town of Haddonfield, as Halloween: The Game was next to be confirmed. The game launches September 8, 2026.Halloween Game Gets A Release Date, Won't Be Here For This HalloweenThe Last EpochEleventh Hour Games's action-RPG The Last Epoch has been confirmed for release on PlayStation 5. The adventure's first expansion, Orobyss, will launch on PS5 the same day as other platforms.Sonic Racing CrossWorldsSega revealed the next downloadable content pack for Sonic Racing: CrossWorlds featuring Mega Man from Capcom! Mega Man, Proto Man, and a new course based on Wily's Fortress will be included in the DLC pack, which launches next year.Nioh 3After a rumored release date leaked earlier today, Koei Tecmo confirmed that Nioh 3 will indeed launch February 6, 2026. The date came as part of a new gameplay trailer that showed off both in-game action and cinematic story beats. Dynasty Warriors 3 Complete Edition RemasteredDeus Ex was not the only remaster to be shown off during the State of Play, as Koei Tecmo followed Nioh 3 with the news that Dynasty Warriors 3 Complete Edition, which also commemorates the 25th anniversary of the original title, is set to launch March 19, 2026.Code Vein IIA new trailer for Bandai Namco's action-RPG Code Vein II was next up. The video showed fresh gameplay of some of the hero's arsenal, including a weapon consisting of wolf-heads on the hero's shoulders that bite and tear into foes. Code Vein II launches January 30, 2026. Let It Die: InfernoThe "extraction-action" game Let It Die returns with Let It Die: Inferno. Players will control customizable raiders and explore a massive crater filled with loot and danger. Let It Die: Inferno launches December 3.Chronoscript: The Endless EndNext, Shuiesha Games unveiled a new 2D adventure with a striking visual style called Chronoscript: The Endless End. The game follows a lost soul who must escape from the pages of an "endless" storybook. Chronoscript is set to launch in 2026. Crimson DesertPearl Abyss's massive RPG adventure Crimson Desert was next to be shown, with fresh gameplay showing off the player-character and many of the warriors he'll join during his quest. Pearl Abyss also confirmed the game's release date: March 19, 2026.Crimson Desert Slated For March 2026 Release DatePulse Elevate Wireless SpeakersNext up was a reveal from PlayStation's audio accessory portfolio, the Pulse Elevate Wireless Speakers. These speakers can be placed anywhere around the player during a gameplay session on PC or PS5, as they support 3D Audio and seamless transitioning between PC, PS5, and even the PlayStation Portal. The Pulse Elevate Wireless Speakers launch in 2026. PlayStation PlusNext was a cavalcade of games heading to the PlayStation Plus subscription service, headlined by Alan Wake 2, which will join the service on October 7. Also coming to the PS Plus lineup are The Last Of Us Part II and two classic fighters from Bandai Namco: Tekken 3 and Soulcalibur III.Gran Turismo 7Next, PlayStation announced that the Gran Turismo series has reached 100 million units sold throughout its lifetime. To celebrate, the next update for Gran Turismo 7, called Spec III, will add two new race tracks, eight new vehicles, and more in December.Gran Turismo 7 Gets Spec III Update In December As Franchise Passes 100 Million UnitsGod of War 20th Anniversary DualSenseA new DualSense controller celebrating the 20th anniversary of God of Was was unveiled next. The controller is completely white, save for the red stripe calling back to Kratos's famous markings on the right side.Marvel's WolverineThe final announcement of the State of Play was a first look at Marvel's Wolverine, the next superhero title from Insomniac Games. The ultra-bloody gameplay was coupled with the reveal of Liam McIntyre as the voice of Wolverine.Marvel's Wolverine Trailer Reveals 2026 Release Window And Very Bloody Gameplay
