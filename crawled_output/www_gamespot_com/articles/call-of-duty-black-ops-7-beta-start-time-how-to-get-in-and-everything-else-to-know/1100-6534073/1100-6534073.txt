Call Of Duty: Black Ops 7 Beta: Start Date, How To Get In, And Everything Else To KnowA multiplayer beta for the upcoming shooter is coming soon.By

A multiplayer beta for Call of Duty: Black Ops 7 has been announced and begins relatively soon, giving players a first chance to try out the game's new features, including wall-jumping and more.Here's everything you need to know about the upcoming Black Ops 7 multiplayer beta.When does the Black Ops 7 beta start?The Black Ops 7 multiplayer beta starts on October 2 and runs through October 8, although not everyone will be able to begin playing right away on October 2.The Black Ops 7 beta is coming sorta soon.Specifically, the Black Ops 7 beta begins at 10 AM PT / 1 PM ET on Thursday, October 2 in early access, before the beta opens for everyone at 10 AM PT / 1 PM ET on Sunday, October 5. It ends on Wednesday, October 8 at 10 AM PT / 1 PM ET.File size informationOn September 2, Activision announced that the file size for the upcoming Black Ops 6 and Warzone Season 5 Reloaded update will be "larger on some platforms." This is due to how Activision is "reorganizing" some Call of Duty content to prepare for the Black Ops 7 beta.How to get in to the Black Ops 7 betaThe Black Ops 7 multiplayer beta is free and open to everyone everywhere, but people who subscribe to a "select" Game Pass subscription tier can start early on October 2. Additionally, people who preorder Black Ops 7 can start playing the beta on October 2. The beta opens for everyone on October 5.If you preorder a copy of Black Ops 7 digitally, you don't need a code. However, if you preorder a physical copy of Black Ops 7 for console, you can get a code at retailer you ordered from. Preordering is not required to gain entry to the beta, only to gan access to the early access period.Another way to access the Black Ops 7 beta is to be an Xfinity subscriber--members can navigate to the Rewards hub of their account where they should find a code granting them access to the beta. What's in the Black Ops 7 beta?Activision has not announced what maps and modes will be in the Black Ops 7 multiplayer beta. It's likely the company is saving more announcements for Call of Duty Next in September.Black Ops 7 takes place in the year 2035 and will feature futuristic weapons and gear. New for Black Ops 7 is a multiplayer mode called Skirmish, which is a 20v20 mode feature multiple objectives. Also in this mode players can use wingsuits and grappling hooks, along with vehicles in what's described as a large-scale mode. Additionally, Black Ops 7 has a new "Overlock" system that allows players to further augment their loadouts.Additionally, the Omnimovement system from Black Ops 6 is back in Black Ops 7, and it's beefed up with a wall-jumping ability.Black Ops 7 will have more than a dozen maps at launch along with 30 weapons, but exactly how many maps, modes, and weapons will be in the beta is unknown. Perhaps Activision is saving further announcements for Call of Duty Next in September.
