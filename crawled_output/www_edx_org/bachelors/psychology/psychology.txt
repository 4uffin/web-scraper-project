As in any other field with such diverse career outcomes, the salary of anyone with a BA in psychology will vary depending on their chosen field, geographic location, population served, employer, experience level, and on whether they hold graduate degrees, too. It also comes down to the type of professional role one holds or wants to hold.The median annual wage for substance abuse, behavioral disorder, and mental health counselors in 2024, for instance, as reported by the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), was $59,190. When comparing a BA vs BS in psychology, it is important to keep your own professional goals in mind. The two degrees offer a similar level of rigor, and both qualify you for entrance into advanced degree programs in psychology.Both degrees also provide a strong foundation for careers in psychology; however, a bachelor of arts in psychology and a bachelor of science in psychology emphasize different elements of the field.A BA in psychology is a more general degree, with a wider range of elective opportunities and an emphasis on the humanities and social sciences. With this degree, many graduates immediately enter the workforce, often in fields adjacent to psychology such as law, political science, or counseling. Graduates may also pursue an advanced degree and licensure in a range of fields including psychology, social work, and counseling. Conversely, a BS in psychology concentrates on research, analytics, and science.
