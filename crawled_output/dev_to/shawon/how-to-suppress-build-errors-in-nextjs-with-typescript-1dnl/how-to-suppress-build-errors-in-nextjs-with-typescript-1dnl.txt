How To Suppress Build Errors in Next.js with TypeScript
#nextjs
#typescript
#webdev

In the fast-paced world of web development, minimizing roadblocks during prototyping or initial integration can be useful. Suppressing build errors is one way to keep your pipeline moving, especially when integrating large third-party libraries. Track suppressed errors: Maintain a list of known issues to avoid introducing new bugs.
Code review required: All uses of suppressed errors should be reviewed, and a plan for resolution should be in place.
