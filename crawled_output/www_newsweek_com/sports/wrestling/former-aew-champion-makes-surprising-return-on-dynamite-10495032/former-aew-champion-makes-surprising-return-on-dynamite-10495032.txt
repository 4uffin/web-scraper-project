WrestlingNews ArticleFormer AEW Champion Makes Surprising Return on DynamitePublishedSep 24, 2025 at 09:17 PM EDTupdatedSep 24, 2025 at 09:18 PM EDTBy Fernando Quiles JrContributing Sports WriterShareNewsweek is a Trust Project memberOne of AEW's top stars made his return on Wednesday's episode of "Dynamite." A six-man tag team match between The Conglomeration and The Don Callis Family was booked inside the Petersen Events Center in Pittsburgh. While Callis revealed the members of his team, Mark Briscoe and Hologram had a mystery partner. Briscoe teased the partner in question prior to the match, and it was indeed Orange Cassidy.Callis was set to unveil his latest painting, but Cassidy destroyed the artwork to make his own grandiose entrance. Rocky Romero attempted to ambush Cassidy, but he ended up crashing into the busted painting.Loading twitter content...Cassidy, Briscoe, and Hologram shared the ring with AEW Continental Champion Kazuchika Okada, Konosuke Takeshita, and Hechicero. Cassidy has had history with all three of his opponents. He took on Okada, Takeshita, and even Briscoe for Okada's gold during the "All Out 2024" pay-per-view. During the March 12 episode of "AEW Dynamite," Cassidy defeated Hechicero in singles action....More News: TNA Victory Road 2025 Match Possibly in Jeopardy Amid InvestigationWhere Has Orange Cassidy Been?Cassidy hadn't had a match since the March 19 episode of "AEW Dynamite." He fell short in a four-way title eliminator that also featured Mike Bailey, Ricochet, and Mark Davis. The former AEW International Champion had been out of action due to a pectoral injury. It was reported that Cassidy's pectoral was torn during his most recent match prior to Wednesday.With Cassidy back in the fold, AEW's active babyface roster has gotten a boost.
