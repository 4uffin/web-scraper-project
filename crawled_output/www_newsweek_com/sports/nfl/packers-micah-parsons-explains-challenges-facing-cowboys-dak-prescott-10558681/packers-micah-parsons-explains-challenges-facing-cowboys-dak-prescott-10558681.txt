NFLDallas CowboysGreen Bay PackersNews ArticlePackers’ Micah Parsons Doesn’t Hold Back on Facing Dak PrescottPublishedSep 25, 2025 at 10:34 AM EDTupdatedSep 25, 2025 at 11:07 AM EDTBy Reice ShipleyContributing Sports WriterShareNewsweek is a Trust Project memberGreen Bay Packers star edge rusher Micah Parsons is looking to show the Dallas Cowboys exactly why they made a huge mistake in trading him when the two teams face off on Sunday night for the first time since the blockbuster deal. But despite all of the animosity that Parsons may have for the Cowboys, he seemingly still has a lot of love for Cowboys star quarterback Dak Prescott.Parsons and Prescott played together for the entirety of Parsons' four-year NFL career. And in that time, Parsons has learned the ins and outs of how his former teammate goes about playing the quarterback position....More news: Commanders’ Jayden Daniels Doesn’t Hold Back on Week 4 Injury StatusWhile most in Parsons' position may finally relish the chance to go after his longtime teammate now that he finally has the chance to, Parsons shared all of the positive elements of Prescott's game that he and the Packers defense need to worry about."I think Dak is so good because he’s overly confident," said Parsons while speaking with reporters on Wednesday, via Cowboys beat reporter Jon Machota. "He believes he can make every throw. And I think 90% of the time he does do that. You want your quarterback to have that mentality. I just think he’s a person who is very resilient and he’s determined. I never would bet against him.”Loading twitter content...More news: Patriots’ Mike Vrabel Shares Major Injury Update Ahead of Week 4Parsons and the Packers defense will need to find a way to use Prescott's "overconfidence" against him and potentially turn those kinds of throws into turnovers. Fortunately for the Packers, those throws have turned into interceptions early on in the 2025 season, with three interceptions thrown in the first three games.Despite the fact that Parsons clearly has a lot of admiration and respect for Prescott, he has also made it clear in the lead-up to Sunday's game that he plans on making the matchup a "painful" experience for him.So it will be interesting to see whether Parsons can get his revenge against the team that traded him, or if the Cowboys and Prescott will get the last laugh.
