Two people were killed and five others were injured in an early morning shooting in Indianapolis on Sunday, according to the Indianapolis Metropolitan Police Department (IMPD).Newsweek has reached out to the IMPD by email on Sunday morning for more information.Why It MattersSunday's shooting has added to a growing tally of gun violence incidents affecting local communities across the country and an intense 24-hours in Indianapolis.The shooting came after IMPD officers encountered a 34-year-old man at a McDonald's on Saturday evening who was armed with a gun, threatening employees. When officers approached the man, it led to an officer-involved shooting, according to a press release from the department. The suspect was not struck by the gunfire and was taken into custody after he fled the fast-food restaurant.What To KnowIMPD officers found five people suffering from gunshot wounds when they responded to a report of gunfire at the 6800 block of Shore Terrace after 2 a.m. local time on Sunday.Two people were pronounced dead on the scene while three others were transported to the hospital, according to a press release from the IMPD. Two more victims self-transported themselves to a hospital and to a fire station, the release said.Three victims are in critical condition while the other two are in serious condition, local news station WTHR reported.A preliminary investigation revealed a disturbance preceded the shooting that took place in a parking lot, but no further information has been given about the suspect or suspects.Homicide detectives and forensic investigators are gathering evidence as part of IMPD's investigation.

What People Are SayingIMPD Chief Chris Bailey said in part in a statement emailed to Newsweek on Sunday: "My heart is heavy after the violence the Indianapolis community experienced over the last 24 hours. Our thoughts are with the victims, their loved ones, and all who are impacted by the violence. IMPD officers and detectives have been working nonstop to identify those responsible and hold them accountable. I am incredibly proud of their courage and professionalism during such a difficult night."He added: "During one of these incidents, two of our officers were attacked while responding to a disturbance involving a man with a gun, and a shot was fired in their direction. Their quick, decisive actions and their training brought the situation to a safe resolution. No one was injured. We are ensuring both officers have access to all available support and resources. We understand how nights like this can shake our sense of safety, but IMPD remains fully engaged and continues to do our part to keep the neighborhoods safe."What Happens Next?The IMPD stated that detectives will continue to interview witnesses and collect evidence to determine the sequence of events that led to the fatal shooting.The Marion County Coroner's Office is expected to release the names of the deceased after notifying their families.Police asked anyone with information, photographs, or video footage from the area to come forward and reach out to Detective Christopher Winter at the IMPD Homicide Office at 317-327-3475, Christopher.Winter@indy.gov, or contact Crime Stoppers of Central Indiana anonymously at 317-262-TIPS (8477) or through www.CrimeTips.org.
