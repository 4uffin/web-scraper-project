PoliticsChicagoICEDepartment of Homeland SecurityKristi NoemTrump administrationNews ArticleICE Agent Described Injuries Trump’s DHS Called Serious as ‘Nothing Major’PublishedSep 23, 2025 at 06:04 PM EDTupdatedSep 23, 2025 at 07:33 PM EDT...By Gabe WhisnantBreaking News EditorShareNewsweek is a Trust Project memberA federal immigration agent described his injuries as “nothing major” in body camera footage released Tuesday, even as Trump administration officials had said he was seriously hurt after a confrontation that ended with the fatal shooting of a Mexican immigrant earlier this month.The footage, captured by a local officer in Franklin Park, shows agents moments after an immigration enforcement officer shot and killed 38-year-old Silverio Villegas González, who allegedly tried to flee a traffic stop and dragged one of the agents with his car. Prosecutors and investigators have not released additional details, citing an active investigation. ICE’s acting enforcement director, Marcos Charles, told The Associated Press that he visited the injured officer in the hospital and believed the use of force was justified. He declined to elaborate further....Why It MattersThe incident, which occurred in the majority Hispanic suburb west of Chicago, has heightened tensions in a city already grappling with a stepped-up federal immigration crackdown, dubbed "Operation Midway Blitz" that officials say has led to nearly 550 arrests.The Franklin Park incident follows similar clashes during immigration raids in other cities. Earlier this summer, ICE operations in Los Angeles led to two deaths when immigrants tried to escape arrest — one man fell from a greenhouse roof, and another was struck by a vehicle outside a Home Depot store.What to KnowVillegas González, a father of young children and a cook in Franklin Park, had just dropped off one of his children at day care the morning of the shooting, according to community members. Witnesses and neighbors later gathered at vigils, describing him as a kind man devoted to his family. The Mexican consulate in Chicago identified him as a native of Michoacán and said it would “closely monitor” the investigation.The Department of Homeland Security has said Villegas González drove his car toward officers, dragging one before the agent opened fire. Agents were not equipped with body cameras, making the Franklin Park Police Department’s footage one of the few public records of the encounter. In the video, one agent tells a local officer, “He tried to run us over,” while another concedes he was only “dragged a little bit.”Federal officials had initially described the injuries to the immigration officer as severe. Homeland Security Secretary Kristi Noem wrote on X that the officer’s “life was put at risk and he sustained serious injuries,” while other officials emphasized that the officer had been hospitalized. But the newly released video shows the injured agent minimizing his condition, telling colleagues his wounds amounted to a knee injury and some hand lacerations. He then shrugs his arms and says, “Nothing major.”That discrepancy has fueled questions about the government’s account of the shooting and whether the use of lethal force was justified. Advocates say the footage undermines claims that agents acted out of fear for their lives, while federal officials insist an ongoing investigation will clarify what happened.What People Are SayingRep. Jesús “Chuy” García, a Chicago Democrat, is among the elected officials ramping up pressure for transparency: “We want answers to questions that we have raised, The family is entitled to it. The community wants to know what is going on, and the public deserves answers as well.”Silverio Villegas-Gonzalez's family said in their fundraising message: "Silverio passed away after ICE agents shot him fatally, leaving behind not only loving family and friends, but also a legacy of warmth, resilience, and deep compassion. He was someone who always extended a helping hand, shared his smile freely, and showed up for those he loved—no matter the circumstances."DHS Assistant Secretary Tricia McLaughlin said in a September 14 statement: "We are praying for the speedy recovery of our law enforcement officer. He followed his training, used appropriate force, and properly enforced the law to protect the public and law enforcement. Viral social media videos and activists encouraging illegal aliens to resist law enforcement not only spread misinformation, but also undermine public safety, as well as the safety of our officers and those being apprehended."What Happens NextThe Department of Homeland Security has not commented on the newly released video, and ICE has said it will await the results of an internal review. Local and federal investigations are expected to determine whether the shooting was lawful and whether agents followed proper protocols during the traffic stop.Immigrant rights groups have pledged to continue demonstrations in Franklin Park, calling for federal agents to wear body cameras and for greater oversight of immigration operations in communities of color.
