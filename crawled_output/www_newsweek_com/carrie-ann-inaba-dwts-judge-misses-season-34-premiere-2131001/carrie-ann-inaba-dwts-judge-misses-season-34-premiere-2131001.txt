EntertainmentDancing With the StarsDWTSJudgeReality TVIllnessNews ArticleWhere is Carrie Ann Inaba? ‘Dancing With the Stars’ Judge Misses PremierePublishedSep 17, 2025 at 03:33 AM EDTupdatedSep 17, 2025 at 05:09 AM EDTBy Megan CartwrightDeputy Entertainment EditorShareNewsweek is a Trust Project memberLongtime Dancing With the Stars (DWTS) judge Carrie Ann Inaba was noticeably missing from the Season 34 premiere on Tuesday.Her absence reduced the judging panel to Bruno Tonioli and Derek Hough for the live kickoff of the show's 20th anniversary season on ABC and Disney+. Producers did not name a guest judge for the episode.Newsweek reached out to Inaba's representative and an ABC spokesperson for comment on Wednesday via email outside regular working hours.Why It MattersInaba is an original and long-serving judge on Dancing With the Stars. Earlier this year, the Hawaii native marked two decades with the series....What To KnowInaba, 57, posted on Instagram shortly before the Season 34 premiere. "I wanted to share that I will not be at the show tonight as I am feeling quite under the weather," she told her 753,000 followers. "I'm resting up and can't wait to be back next week in good health.""In the meantime, I wish all the contestants the very best of luck. I'll be cheering you on from home. Have a great show everyone!"
View this post on Instagram
A post shared by Carrie Ann Inaba (@carrieanninaba)In June, Inaba celebrated her 20th anniversary with the program in a lengthy message posted to social media.She said at the time,"20 years ago today, I sat down for the first time with two men I had never met before. Len Goodman and @brunotonioliofficial. My life was forever changed.""It's been an incredible journey of self discovery and a wonderful experience witnessing the unique intersection of dance and human nature, competition and shared experience, artistry and vulnerability. There has been nothing like it in my life."She added: "I greatly appreciate having a 'seat at the table' (as they say) to share my thoughts, my truths, and my own personal experience to help shape the experience of others towards dance."...Season 34—which is co-hosted by Alfonso Ribeiro and Julianne Hough—welcomed 14 new celebrities and their professional partners to the ballroom. Contestants include Australian TV presenter Robert Irwin and Witney Carson, Olympic gymnast Jordan Chiles and Ezra Sosa, The Traitors star Dylan Efron and Daniella Karagach, Boy Meets World alum Danielle Fishel and Pasha Pashkov, and Yoga Vida co-founder and author Hilaria Baldwin and Gleb Savchenko.What People Are SayingIn the comments underneath Inaba's Instagram post, people sent well-wishes to the TV personality.Hilaria Baldwin said: "Feel better."ABC News meteorologist Ginger Zee wrote: "Sending healing energy!"Double DWTS champion Julianne Hough posted: "Get better soon sis."Former DWTS pro Peta Murgatroyd added: "Feel better babe :("Season 34 professional dancer Emma Slater shared: "Oh my love, get better soon. Sending love."What Happens NextThe circumstances of Inaba's illness beyond her Instagram statement were not disclosed by the judge or her representatives as of publication.While no one was eliminated during week one, there will be a double exit next week.Dancing With the Stars Season 34 will air and stream simultaneously on ABC and Disney+ on Tuesdays.
