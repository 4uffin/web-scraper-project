Clues frequently reference pop culture and historical events that are part of public knowledge. When contestants fail to provide an accepted response, it often prompts a discussion from viewers at home....What To KnowOn September 15, Sesame Street character Oscar the Grouch read off the $400 question: "Throughout the years, lots of people have stopped by to visit Sesame Street, like Alex Trebek and Ken Jennings," he said in a clip posted to Jeopardy!'s official Instagram account. "Why can't they leave a grouch alone? But, the very first was this guy, who recited the alphabet for us."When the buzzer sounded following silence from all three contestants, host Jennings said: "That's James Earl Jones!"
View this post on Instagram
A post shared by Jeopardy! (@jeopardy)Jeopardy!'s question comes just over one year after Jones' death on September 9, 2024. He was 93.Jones rose to fame on Broadway before transitioning to film. The actor was nominated for an Academy Award for 1970s The Great White Hope, where he starred as boxer Jack Jefferson. He's best known, however, for his roles as Darth Vader in the Star Wars franchise and Mufasa in The Lion King.Jones was the first celebrity guest to appear on Jeopardy! in 1969. According to the children's television show, "his slow, steady delivery had such an impact on children that it was dubbed 'the James Earl Jones effect,' showing that repetition and anticipation could promote interactive learning."What People Are SayingIn the comments underneath Jeopardy!'s Instagram post, people were quick to call out the contestants.@mrk131982 said in a reply with 3,062 likes: "Nobody knew James Earl Jones, straight to jail."@americanmegalo agreed, writing in a remark with 1,169 likes: "The way I screamed JAMES EARL JONES to my phone multiple times."@traceybaptistewrites shared in a message with 745 likes: "How do they not know Monsterpiece Theater and James Earl Jones?
