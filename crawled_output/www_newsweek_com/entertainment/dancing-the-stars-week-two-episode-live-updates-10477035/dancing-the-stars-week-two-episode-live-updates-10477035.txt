What to knowJudging on Dancing With the Stars has always sparked debate, but few rules stir more conversation than the infamous “lift rule.” Enforced primarily by judge Carrie Ann Inaba, the rule prohibits lifts in traditional ballroom and Latin dances—defined as any moment when a dancer has both feet off the ground with help from their partner.The rule, rooted in National Dance Council of America standards, aims to preserve the integrity of competitive ballroom technique and ensure fairness across all age groups and abilities. Carrie Ann has repeatedly clarified that she didn’t create the rule, but she’s committed to upholding it, even when it means docking points.Lifts are permitted in styles such as Jitterbug, Argentine Tango, Charleston, Salsa, Jazz, and Contemporary.
