NewsManhuntBreaking NewsWashington stateNews ArticleTravis Decker Update: Prosecutors Ask Judge to Drop Arrest WarrantPublishedSep 24, 2025 at 01:29 PM EDTupdatedSep 24, 2025 at 02:40 PM EDTBy Gabe Whisnant and Hollie SilvermanShareNewsweek is a Trust Project memberFederal prosecutors have asked a judge to drop the arrest warrant for a former soldier accused of killing his three young daughters after the U.S. Marshals Service said he is believed to be dead.Authorities last week discovered remains in a remote area of central Washington believed to be those of 32-year-old Travis Decker, though DNA tests are still pending.Prosecutors said in a court filing Wednesday that the Marshals Service advised them that Decker is believed dead. Decker had been the subject of a three-month manhunt after his daughters were found suffocated in June at a campground near Leavenworth.Newsweek reached out to the Chelan County Sheriff's office for further comment.Why It MattersThe announcement came after months of searching for Decker following the discovery of his deceased daughters at a Washington State campground in early June.The discovery marked the end of a month-long search that spanned multiple states, rugged wilderness, as well as trails and campsites in the Pacific Northwest.Who Is Travis Decker?Decker was an Army veteran who was considered highly skilled in wilderness survival and the subject of a monthslong manhunt.He was the father of three daughters who died in June after they had never returned from a visitation with him.Local authorities found the girls deceased after an Amber alert and then spent months looking for him across rugged terrain and campsites in the Pacific Northwest.Decker was deployed in 2014 to Afghanistan while on active duty in the Army. He was then transferred to the Washington National Guard in 2021, according to a spokesperson for the Washington State Military, as reported by ABC.Three-Month Manhunt Ends After Remains FoundAuthorities found human remains during a search for Decker that included the U.S. Marshals Service Pacific Northwest Violent Offender Task Force, the Chelan County Sheriff’s Office, Washington State Patrol, US Border Patrol, U.S. Forrest Service, Spokane County Sheriff’s Office, and the FBI."During the search, human remains were located in a remote wooded area south of the town of Leavenworth," according to a release from the Chelan County Sheriff's Office, which added "preliminary findings suggest the remains belong to Travis Decker."His remains were sent in for forensic testing following the discovery that marked the end of a multi-agency search that lasted several months.What to Know About Travis Decker's Three DaughtersThe three children—Paityn, 9; Evelyn, 8; and Olivia, 5—were reported missing on May 30 after they did not return from a scheduled visit with their father. Their bodies were discovered on June 2 near the Rock Island Campground.The cause of death was determined to be suffocation, and the manner of death to be homicide.Investigators reported that the girls had been bound with zip ties and had plastic bags placed over their heads.Mother of Travis Decker's Three Daughters Speaks Out Following Their DeathsA memorial service was held in late June for Paityn, 9; Evelyn, 8; and Olivia, 5. Their mother, Whitney Decker, thanked the attendees as she remembered her daughters, saying, "I truly hope that the legacy of the girls' lives lives on in everyone's heart forever. They were incredible," People reported."I'm so thankful for that time I had with the girls," Whitney said of bedtime, adding that, "When the world slowed down, it gave me an inside look at their day," according to the report."I believe doing this with them allowed them to come into the world with open hearts and kindness."What Happens NextAuthorities will reveal the results of the forensic testing when available.It is unclear if law enforcement will pursue any additional actions following the discovery of the remains.Update 9/24/2025 2:13 p.m. ET: This story has been updated to include more information This article includes reporting by the Associated Press.
