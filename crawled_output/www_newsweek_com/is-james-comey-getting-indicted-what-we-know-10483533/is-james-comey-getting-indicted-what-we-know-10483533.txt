What We KnowPublishedSep 24, 2025 at 05:27 PM EDT...By Hollie Silverman and Jenna SundelShareNewsweek is a Trust Project memberFormer FBI Director James Comey could face perjury charges due to alleged false statements made to Congress during a 2020 testimony, according to reports from multiple media outlets.Federal prosecutors are still deciding whether to bring those charges forward as the legal deadline looms, CNN reported, citing people familiar with the situation.Although prosecutors haven't found sufficient evidence to charge Comey, the US Attorney in Virginia plans to ask a grand jury to indict him, according to a report from ABC News.The possible charges stem from alleged false statements Comey made while giving testimony on possible Russian interference in the 2016 presidential election.Comey, who was first appointed by former President Barack Obama, was fired by President Donald Trump for his handling of an investigation into former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton's use of her personal email.Since his firing, Comey has been a critic of Trump and his administration.This is a breaking news story.
