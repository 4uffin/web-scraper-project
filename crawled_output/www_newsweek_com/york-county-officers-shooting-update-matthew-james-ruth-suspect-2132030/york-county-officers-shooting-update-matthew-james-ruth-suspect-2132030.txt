PoliticsPennsylvaniaBreaking NewsCrimeGun ViolenceJosh ShapiroNews ArticleMatthew James Ruth Identified as Gunman in Fatal Pennsylvania ShootingPublishedSep 18, 2025 at 12:24 PM EDTupdatedSep 18, 2025 at 06:04 PM EDTBy Gabe WhisnantBreaking News EditorShareNewsweek is a Trust Project memberA gunman who killed three police officers and wounded two others in rural southern Pennsylvania before being shot dead by police was a 24-year-old man wanted on stalking charges, authorities said Thursday.The suspect, identified to the Associated Press as Matthew James Ruth, was also facing charges of trespassing, loitering and prowling at night in a domestic-related case that began a day before the deadly violence, according to court records.The confrontation erupted Wednesday in York County as officers closed in on Ruth near his family's farm outside Hanover....What We Know About the VictimsDetective Sgt. Cody Becker, Detective Mark Baker and Detective Isaiah Emenheiser were the officers who were killed, York County District Attorney Tim Barker said. They all worked for the Northern York County Regional Police Department.One of the wounded officers serves with the Northern York Regional Police Department, while authorities have not released the agency affiliation of the second hospitalized officer. Investigators also discovered the ex-girlfriend's dog shot to death in the basement.Across the region, police departments have expressed their condolences on social media, while mourners left flowers outside the Northern York Regional Police Department headquarters. As word of Wednesday's tragedy spread, residents lined the streets with American flags, saluting as a procession of police and emergency vehicles accompanied the fallen officers to the coroner's office."Each of these men represented the best of policing. They served with professionalism, dedication and courage. They were leaders within our agency, committed to protecting this community and standing beside their follow officers," Chief Dave Lash said.Pennsylvania Police Shooting: What We KnowAuthorities say Ruth ambushed officers who came to arrest him at his ex-girlfriend's home in rural York County on Wednesday afternoon. Police opened the unlocked door around 2:10 p.m. and were met with gunfire from Ruth, who was armed with an AR-style rifle fitted with a suppressor, according to Barker.Officials said Ruth fired multiple rounds at the four officers at the door. A gunfight then ensued between Ruth and two officers outside. Ruth wounded a sheriff's deputy before police shot the gunman to death.Who is alleged gunman Matthew James Ruth?Ruth had been sought on misdemeanor stalking, prowling, and trespassing charges after his ex-girlfriend and her mother reported seeing a man in camouflage peering into the home with binoculars Tuesday night. A trail camera image also appeared to show Ruth outside the property with a rifle and scope.Ruth eluded police that night, leading them to return Wednesday with misdemeanor warrants for his arrest. The young woman's car had been set on fire in August and she said she suspected he may have been involved.Further details on the domestic dispute are expected as investigators process evidence removed from the area around Ruth's home.Rose Miller, who lives a few doors down, said investigators first arrived around noon Wednesday and returned later in the afternoon. She said she did not know Ruth well but remembered him selling items for Boy Scouts fundraisers.Police removed bags of evidence from the home and left just after midnight, she said.
