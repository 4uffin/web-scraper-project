WorldFlying CarsChinaCar crashWorld NewsNews ArticleWATCH: Flying Cars Crash in ChinaPublishedSep 17, 2025 at 03:08 AM EDTupdatedSep 17, 2025 at 04:29 AM EDTBy John Feng, Robert Birsel, and Shane CroucherShareNewsweek is a Trust Project memberTwo flying cars collided during a rehearsal for the Changchun Air Show in China, the manufacturer XPeng AeroHT said, blaming "insufficient flight distance."Newsweek has contacted XPeng AeroHT, which is an affiliate of Chinese electric vehicle (EV) maker Xpeng, via an online form seeking comment.Why It MattersThe incident highlights some of the safety concerns around flying vehicles, with flying taxis set to take to the skies soon.The crash of the two electric flying cars could be an embarrassment for the Chinese EV sector, but it is unlikely to stall its advance....What To KnowThe two electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft (eVTOLs) crashed on Tuesday, September 16."One landed safely, while the other caught fire upon landing due to body damage," XPeng AeroHT said in a statement, Chinese business publication Yicai reported."All on-site personnel were safe, and competent agencies have completed the on-site handling work orderly, with the specific cause for the accident still under investigation."AeroHT announced in March last year that its Voyager X2 had completed a low-altitude flight in the central business district of the southern Chinese city of Guangzhou, Reuters reported at the time.The Voyager X2 put on a demonstration flight in the capital, Beijing, in June last year, the news agency reported.XPeng AeroHT said its website it is developing 200 "flying camps" where people can get experience of their vehicles. It did not say if any camps would be set up outside China.China is the world's EV powerhouse, pushing the development and adoption of the technology at home and abroad—China's BYD beat Tesla to become the world's top EV car maker in 2023.BYD and other Chinese automakers are expanding across the globe, offering relatively affordable EVs with sleek designs and high-tech features.What People Are SayingXPeng AeroHT said on its website: "Strategically developing flying camps around cities and popular self-driving routes equipped with takeoff and landing platforms and clear airspace.
